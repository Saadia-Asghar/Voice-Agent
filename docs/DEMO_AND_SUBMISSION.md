# BenchDial Demo and Submission Package

Official judging frame: **creativity, technical depth, presentation**. Make one memorable claim, prove the signed evidence chain on screen, and keep a single story.

## One-line pitch

BenchDial turns one verified laboratory repair brief into comparable AI voice negotiations and an evidence-backed recovery decision.

## Why it is not a wrapper

- ScopePrint makes every provider quote the same canonical specification.
- The Honesty Firewall validates competing-bid leverage outside the model.
- Structured endings prevent vague calls from becoming fake quotes.
- The Concession Ledger proves what changed, why, and which transcript turn supports it.
- Downtime-adjusted ranking exposes operational cost hidden by headline prices.

## Submission copy

### Problem

Laboratories lose money twice when equipment fails: through downtime and through incomparable phone quotes that omit necessary work.

### Solution

BenchDial creates one voice-and-document ScopePrint, gives every provider that identical specification, itemizes every commercial and operational term, negotiates only with verified leverage, and produces an evidence-linked award memo.

### Technical implementation

React and TypeScript provide the four-stage interface. ElevenLabs Agents power voice intake and negotiation. Supabase Auth protects billable live mode; Edge Functions mint private conversation tokens and verify post-call HMAC webhooks. PostgreSQL stores canonical scopes, provenance, transcripts, analysis, and an idempotent evidence ledger. Deterministic TypeScript ranks cash and downtime-adjusted outcomes.

### Responsible AI

The agent discloses that it is AI, never invents bids or authority, refuses binding purchase actions, records unknown values explicitly, and distinguishes live recordings from simulated fixtures.

## Completion gate

The software and narrative are ready for infrastructure wiring. The entry becomes challenge-complete only after production auth and functions are configured, three real consenting calls are persisted, and one genuine leverage-caused concession is linked in the production database.
