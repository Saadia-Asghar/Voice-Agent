Problem

What we solved

Phone-priced markets hide the real cost until someone has the stamina to call five to eight operators. The Negotiator brief documents this for movers — identical work, 5.6× quote spreads, sight-unseen estimates that blow up later.

The same failures hit laboratory equipment repair:

Opaque pricingIncomparable quotesCall-out, calibration, warranty, and response assumptions differ wildly.
Unreliable estimatesSight-unseen riskIncomplete intake is why final bills jump after commitment.
No way to shopOne call at a timeNobody has hours to run three honest negotiations while a centrifuge is down.
  • Labs pay twice: experiment downtime and a misleading lowball.
  • Software historically cannot dial the market — until voice agents.

Solution & flow

What the app does

STEP 1EstimatorElevenLabs voice interview + service report → shared JSON job spec. User confirms ScopePrint before any call.
STEP 2CallerSame ScopePrint to OEM, hidden-fee, and stonewaller styles. Outcomes: quote, negotiated terms, or decline.
STEP 3CloserVerified competing-bid leverage, 30% red-flag rule, downtime-adjusted ranking, concession ledger.
STEP 4AwardPlain-language recommend with transcript receipts. Human must approve — app cannot purchase.

Differentiation

Why this is a negotiator — not a form

Honesty Firewall No invented bids Server-side check_leverage must verify a competing term before the agent may speak it.
Provenance labels LIVE vs FIXTURE Fixtures never become RECORDED LIVE RUN. Only a signed webhook with a transcript can.
ScopePrint Identical job every call Voice and documents converge before dialing — configuration for the vertical, not a rewrite.
Human boundary Recommend, don’t bind Award memo requires review. BenchDial cannot purchase or accept a contract.

Stack & ask

How it is built

  • UI: React + TypeScript + Vite — Estimator → Caller → Closer → Award
  • Voice: ElevenLabs Agents (intake + Buyer/Closer) over WebRTC
  • Backend: Supabase Auth, Edge Functions (tokens, HMAC webhooks, call-tools), Postgres evidence ledger
  • Logic: Zod contracts, deterministic ranking, 30% below-median red-flag rule outside the LLM
